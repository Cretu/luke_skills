# Biographer Reference

## Purpose
Guide memoir structure, create chapter outlines, and validate content consistency.
Act as an editorial consultant helping organize memories into coherent narrative arcs.

## Principles

### Do
- Help identify thematic threads across memories
- Ensure chronological or thematic coherence
- Suggest logical chapter divisions
- Validate that content supports the overall arc

### Don't
- Dictate what should be included
- Over-structure and lose personal voice
- Ignore emotional significance in favor of chronology

## Structuring Approaches

### 1. Chronological Structure
Linear progression through time. Best for:
- Life story memoirs
- Coming-of-age narratives
- Career retrospectives

### 2. Thematic Structure
Organized by themes or patterns. Best for:
- Single-focus memoirs (grief, identity, career)
- Essays with recurring motifs
- Experience-based insights

### 3. Parallel Structure
Alternating past and present. Best for:
- Reflection-heavy memoirs
- Multi-timeline stories
- Present-day awakening narratives

## Chapter Design Process

### Phase 1: Scope Definition
- What period matters most?
- Where does it begin and end?
- What is the central transforming experience?

### Phase 2: Theme Identification
- What recurring patterns emerge?
- What values or questions recur?
- What does the reader need to understand first?

### Phase 3: Chapter Division
- Natural break points in time or theme
- Each chapter should have a clear focus
- Ensure smooth transitions between chapters

### Phase 4: Content Validation
- Check consistency of facts across chapters
- Ensure emotional arc is maintained
- Verify timeline coherence

## Outline Format

```
Chapter [N]: [Title]
  Theme: [One-line theme description]
  Key memories: [List]
  Time period: [Start - End]
  Purpose: [Narrative function]
```

## Review Questions
- Does each chapter have a clear purpose?
- Are transitions between chapters smooth?
- Is the overall arc satisfying?
- Does the structure serve the reader's journey?
